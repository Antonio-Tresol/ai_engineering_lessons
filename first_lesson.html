Introduction to Building AI Applications with Foundation Models

AI Engineering Chapter 1 by Huyen (2025)

The Paradigm Shift

Traditional ML Engineering

Build task-specific models from scratch.

Modern AI Engineering

Adapt general-purpose foundation models to specific applications.

"AI engineering—the process of building applications on top of readily available models—[has turned] into one of the fastest-growing engineering disciplines."

- Huyen (2025, p. 1)

The Engine of Scale: Self-Supervision

Traditional supervised learning required expensive, manually labeled data. Self-supervision removed this bottleneck.

The model learns from the data itself:

The quick brown fox...

Input (Context): "The quick brown"

Label (Prediction): "fox"

"Self-supervised learning means that language models can learn from text sequences without requiring any labeling...allowing language models to scale up to become LLMs."

- Huyen (2025, p. 8)

The New Job: Adapting, Not Building

Since we don't pre-train foundation models (it's too expensive), the work of an AI Engineer is model adaptation.

Prompt Engineering

Giving the model clear instructions and context. This is the starting point for almost every application.

System: You are a helpful assistant...
User: Analyze this data...

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

Giving the model external, up-to-date knowledge to draw from (e.g., a database of your company's documents).

Query → Retrieve → Generate

Finetuning (Weight Changes)

Further training a pre-trained model on a smaller, domain-specific dataset to specialize its behavior, style, or knowledge.

Foundation Model → Domain Data → Specialized Model

Higher cost and complexity, but better performance for specific tasks

"AI engineering is the process of building applications on top of readily available models, rather than building models from scratch."

- Huyen (2025, p. 1)

Enterprise Strategy: Internal-Facing First

Companies deploy internal tools first to manage risk and build expertise before launching external, customer-facing applications.

"Internal applications help companies develop their AI engineering expertise while minimizing the risks associated with data privacy, compliance, and potential catastrophic failures."

- Huyen (2025, p. 19)

Your Role as an AI Engineer

Focus on the Application Layer

Most of your work will live in the "Application Development" layer of the stack: prompt engineering, context construction (RAG), and building user interfaces.

Prompt Engineering

Context Construction (RAG)

User Interfaces

Evaluation is Your Biggest Challenge

Traditional ML Evaluation

Straightforward metrics like accuracy, precision, recall on classification tasks.

accuracy = 92.5%

Generative AI Evaluation

How do you programmatically evaluate if generated content is "good," "creative," or "on-brand"?

❓ Much harder problem

This is a central focus of AI Engineering and a rapidly evolving area.

Master the Tradeoffs

Your job isn't just to build, but to decide how. You'll constantly be asking:

"Is prompt engineering sufficient, or do we need RAG?"

"When is the cost and complexity of finetuning justified?"

"Which model provider gives the best performance/cost/latency for our specific use case?"

Performance ⚖️ Cost ⚖️ Latency

Key Takeaways

1. Self-Supervision Unlocked Scale: This is the key technical innovation that enabled today's massive foundation models.

2. AI Engineering is Model Adaptation: Focus on prompt engineering, RAG, and finetuning rather than building models from scratch.

3. The Workflow is Inverted: AI Engineering is product-first, enabling rapid iteration (`Product → Data → Model`).

4. Your Role: Application Layer + Evaluation: Focus on user interfaces, context construction, and solving the challenge of evaluating generative outputs.

5. Master the Tradeoffs: Balance performance, cost, and latency across different adaptation techniques and model providers.

6. Plan Strategically: Go beyond the demo by evaluating the "why," defining the human-AI role, and setting clear metrics from the start.
